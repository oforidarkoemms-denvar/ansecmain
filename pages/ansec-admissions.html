Our Mission

Forming Leaders of Faith and Excellence

Anum Presbyterian Senior High School (ANSEC) was founded in 1897 under the auspices of the Presbyterian Church of Ghana in Anum, Eastern Region. For over eight decades, we have provided quality secondary education grounded in Christian values, academic discipline, and service to community.

Admission to ANSEC is conducted primarily through the Computerized School Selection and Placement System (CSSPS) administered by the Ghana Education Service after the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE).

Admission Requirements

Who Qualifies for Admission

To be considered for placement at ANSEC, all prospective students must meet the following conditions set by the Ghana Education Service.

1
Completed Junior High School (JHS) — Applicants must have successfully completed the full three-year JHS programme at an accredited basic school in Ghana.
2
Sat for and passed the BECE — Applicants must have written the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE) administered by the West African Examinations Council (WAEC).
3
Placed via the CSSPS system — Admission is through the national Computerized School Selection and Placement System. Students must select ANSEC as a school choice during the selection process.
4
Complete the school's admission formalities — Placed students must report by the official reporting date with all required documents and complete the school's registration and orientation programme.
5
Acceptable BECE aggregate score — While the CSSPS determines placement, students should aim for strong aggregate scores to improve chances of being placed in their preferred programme.
What is the CSSPS?

The Computerized School Selection and Placement System is Ghana's national automated system managed by the Ghana Education Service for placing qualified JHS graduates into Senior High Schools across the country.

Students choose up to six SHS schools in order of preference. The system matches students to schools based on their BECE results and school capacity.

Visit cssps.gov.gh
Important: The school code for Anum Presbyterian Senior High School on the CSSPS platform is EPSAS. Ensure you select it correctly during the school selection process.

Step-by-Step Guide

The Admission Process

From your BECE to your first day at ANSEC — here is everything you need to do, in the right order.

1
Write the BECE
Sit for the Basic Education Certificate Examination administered by WAEC at the end of JHS 3. Your results determine your eligibility and programme placement.
WAEC Examination
2
Select ANSEC on the CSSPS Portal
Log in to the CSSPS portal and include Anum Presbyterian Senior High School (EPSAS) in your list of school choices. You may choose up to six schools in order of preference.
cssps.gov.gh
3
Check Placement Results
Wait for the official CSSPS placement release, typically in September. Check the CSSPS portal or GES website for your placement results and the school you have been assigned to.
CSSPS Portal
4
Print Your Placement Form
If placed at ANSEC, download and print your official CSSPS placement letter from the portal. This document is required for registration and must be presented upon reporting.
Official Document
5
Report to ANSEC
Report to the school on the official reporting date with all required documents and fees. Late reporting may forfeit your placement. Check our website for the exact reporting date.
Anum, Eastern Region
6
Complete Registration & Orientation
Submit all documents, pay applicable fees, and complete the school's registration forms. Attend the mandatory orientation programme to learn school rules, routines, and expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply directly to ANSEC without the CSSPS?
No — all fresh admissions must go through the CSSPS. Direct applications are not accepted except in special transfer cases.

Can I change my programme after placement?
Programme changes may be possible within the first week of school, subject to availability. Contact the Headmaster's office.

Is ANSEC a boarding or day school?
ANSEC is primarily a boarding school. Day student arrangements may be available for local students — contact the school for details.
