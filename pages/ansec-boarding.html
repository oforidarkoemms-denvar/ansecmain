Our Boarding Philosophy

More Than a School —
A Community

"Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old he will not depart from it." — Proverbs 22:6

At ANSEC, boarding is an integral part of our educational mission. Our residential programme extends learning beyond the classroom, building character, independence, and a lifelong sense of community rooted in Presbyterian values.

Every boarding student is known by name, supported by dedicated housemasters and housemistresses, and guided by our chaplaincy team — ensuring that spiritual, emotional, and academic needs are met around the clock.

Health & Welfare

Student Health is Our Priority

From routine health checks to emergency referrals, our welfare systems are designed to keep every boarder healthy, safe and thriving.

On-Campus Medical Clinic
A dedicated school clinic staffed by a resident nurse provides first-response care, medication management and health monitoring for all boarding students throughout the term.
Open 7 days a week, 24-hour emergency access
Hospital Referral Network
For cases beyond on-campus care, students are referred to Anum Government Hospital or Koforidua Regional Hospital. Staff escort all students during medical visits.
Anum Government Hospital — 2 km from campus
Nutrition & Dietary Care
Our catering team prepares balanced, varied meals following nutritional guidelines. Special dietary needs and food allergies are accommodated with prior notice to the Welfare Office.
Three meals daily, varied weekly menu

Boarding FAQs

Common Questions

Everything prospective boarding families need to know before admission.

All students in Forms 1–3 who are admitted to ANSEC are eligible for boarding. Boarding is strongly encouraged for students coming from outside Anum and surrounding communities. There is no separate minimum age — eligibility follows standard SHS admission requirements.
House placement is managed by the boarding coordinator based on gender, available space, and other factors. Students may indicate preferences on their admission forms, but the school cannot guarantee specific placements. Requests to change houses are considered on a case-by-case basis.
The official boarding kit list is included in the admission pack. Generally, students should bring: bedding (mattress, sheets, pillow), toiletries, a bucket, school uniforms, sports kit, casual wear, and approved study materials. Electronic devices such as phones and tablets are subject to the school's ICT policy.
Boarding fees cover accommodation, meals, and basic welfare services and are included in the overall school fees structure as regulated by the Ghana Education Service (GES). Any supplementary levies approved by the Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) are communicated officially at the beginning of each academic year.
Night duty staff are always present in the dormitories. In the event of illness, the duty staff will administer first aid, contact the school nurse, and — if necessary — arrange transport to the hospital. Parents are notified promptly for any significant health concern.
Yes. Day students who wish to convert to boarding status may apply to the head of boarding at the beginning of any academic term, subject to available dormitory space. A formal application and parent consent form must be submitted.
